On the Subject of Krooked Slots

Please learn the diffirence between a digit and a number.

A krooked slots module consists of three slots, each with an internal wheel that has six numbers on it. Along with seven buttons that can only be pressed once, pressing all of them is the only method to get the module to strike.

Initially, the slots will diaplay Your number, press the submit button to obtain your Current number. The objective is to use the arrow buttons to rotate the slots such that they collectively display Your solution number.

Firstly, in order to obtain the numbers on each wheel, use the current serial and the following steps; where the first rule corresponds to all slots without a prime position.

  • First take all unevenly positioned characters and use their values for the first-third Second numbers, otherwise for the first four First numbers use their alphanumeric position, otherwise FIX LATER.
  • First from all numbers construct three groups of two single numbers. Second treat them as hexatrigesimal numbers and take the rightmost and second to last significant numbers from their decanary form. Third note that all numbers that aren't in a non-prime position are your Second numbers.
  • First treat all of the numbers as being in unbinbielevenary, second take their first number in reverse reading order from their denary form. First numbers' absolute diffirence from both forms will be greater than π. Third to remove an uneven count of the types, first change the second First number to the non-First type, otherwise first change the first Second number to a First number, never apply a rule that changes the absolute diffirence of the types to a square number.

If possible sort the strings such that their first number is the last occurance of it being indiffirent from Your number's number that's in the position that corresponds to the negative value of the diffirence between lit indicators, unlit indicators and your string's position and if this value is outside the range of -4-4 exclusive then % three.
